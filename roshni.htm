DEDICATED TO WOMEN-CHILD & RURAL DEVELOPMENT.

Roshni (Sanstha) is a non-profit social development charitable Trust registered under the Indian Trust Act by Govt. of NCT of Delhi vide Regd. No. 678 in the year 2014, which is headquartered in Delhi. The organization is also enrolled with NITI Aayog, Govt. of India under NGO Darpan vide Unique ID No. DL/2017/0155613. Moreover, we have been authorized for tax rebates under 12-A & 80-G of the Indian Income Tax Act. Roshni (Sanstha) is a development organization, Our focus has been first and foremost to work on the ground to bring positive change in the lives of deprived society and we have been making equal efforts to sustain this change. Roshni (Sanstha) is envisioned to offer various welfare-based empowerment programs, developmental projects & awareness activities for marginalized sections of the society, villagers and farmers aimed to enrich their educational, health, vocational skill, agricultural, economic, and livelihood avenues along with rural development. We facilitate and focus programs for vulnerable groups like girls, children, the elderly, handicapped, and prison inmates with the sole intention to empower and bring them into the social mainstream. We also perform a variety of humanitarian services and functions. Roshni Trust is authorized to implement its schemes and activities across anywhere in India. During the last 05 years, we have undertaken and accomplished a series of social good activities on education, vocational trainings, health care, sanitation, farming & plantation, which has benefitted thousands of underprivileged youth & children, hundreds of women & farmers/villagers in Delhi-NCR and in some parts of UP & Bihar. The majority of our programs were self-financed or supported by self-generated seeding funds. Our programs were cherished & admired by many valued organizations as well as by several individuals.



There are still many places in India where education has still not reached, it is still a problem. And the places where it has reached the fees of courses are so high that they are not affordable to all.

POSTED ON OCTOBER 24, 2023 BY MANJEET

Noor Kajol likes to stay busy. The 12-year-old fills her days studying hindi, playing games with friends, and making art. A migrant from Rajasthan now living in a ROSHNI-managed camp in Delhi, she left Rajasthan with her family. She has two older sisters and three younger brothers and, unlike so many of children at the camp, her parents are still alive. Her family lives in a small shelter at the camp with no electricity. And although the shelters are hot and become weak after rains, Noor Kajol says this is a better way to live. In Delhi she’s able to continue her schooling, roam around freely, play, and draw whatever she likes. She realizes that had she stayed in Myanmar, she may not have survived, let alone fulfill her dream of becoming a pharmacist.“Girls who complete 10th grade can work in pharmacies. I also want to work in a pharmacy, or become a doctor if possible,” she says.


From Refugee to Pharmacist, this 12-Year-Old is Building a Better Future for Herself.

POSTED ON JULY 30, 2023 BY MANJEET

Noor Kajol likes to stay busy. The 12-year-old fills her days studying Arabic, playing games with friends, and making art. A refugee from Myanmar now living in a CARE-managed camp in Bangladesh, she left Myanmar with her family a year ago in the wave of hundreds of thousands of refugees fleeing extreme violence in the country. She has two older sisters and three younger brothers and, unlike so many of children at the camp, her parents are still alive.
Her family lives in a small shelter at the camp with no electricity. And although the shelters are hot and become weak after rains, Noor Kajol says this is a better way to live. In Bangladesh she’s able to continue her schooling, roam around freely, play, and draw whatever she likes. She realizes that had she stayed in Myanmar, she may not have survived, let alone fulfill her dream of becoming a pharmacist.
